How to read the video

  1. 1
    (a+b)² as area
    Outer square side = a+b on TOP+RIGHT to avoid overlap. Inner a,b on BOTTOM+LEFT. Cut at distance a. Outer fades before inner appears.
  2. 2
    Order: squares → crosses
    a² first, then b², then both ab rectangles together. Same order for 3 variables: a²,b²,c² then ab,ac,bc pairs. Makes the pattern obvious.
  3. 3
    (a+b+c)² = 9 pieces
    Side = a+b+c broken into 3. 3×3 grid → 3 squares + 3×2 rectangles. Right column builds: = a²+b²+c² + ab+ba + ac+ca + bc+cb → a²+b²+c²+2ab+2bc+2ca.
  4. 4
    Pattern
    Every off-diagonal pair appears twice — symmetric across diagonal. General boxed finale stays as end frame.

Final boxed formula

(x₁+x₂+…+xₙ)² = sum xᵢ² + 2 sumi<j xᵢ xⱼ
(x₁+x₂+…+xₙ)² = x₁²+x₂²+…+xₙ² + 2 Σi<j xᵢxⱼ — diagonal = squares, off-diagonal = pairs
diagonal = a²,b²,c² off-diagonal = ab,ac,bc ×2

Why 2ab?

Bottom-left is a². Top-right is b². The other two rectangles are both a×b — one to the right of a², one on top. Same area, two places. So ab+ab=2ab.

Same reason for (a+b+c)²: ab appears at (row a, col b) and (row b, col a) — symmetric across diagonal.

Notation

x₁, x₂, …, xₙ with proper subscripts, xᵢ, xⱼ also subscript, ² superscript everywhere — no ^2 mix. Implemented with Pango <sub> markup to avoid font gaps.
